The Waste Issue

There is too much waste on this planet!

History of Waste

In the United States, the generation of waste increased from 88.1 million tons in 1960 to 292.4 million tons in 2018. In 1960, the generation rate was just 2.68 pounds per person per day. In 1980, it increased to 3.66 pounds per person per day. Then in 2000, it reached 4.74 pounds per person per day, and then decreased to 4.69 pounds per person per day in 2005. In 2018, the generation rate was 4.9 pounds per person per day, an 8% increase from 2017. Despite the massive increase, landfilling of waste has decreased from 94% of the amount generated in 1960 to 50% of the amount generated in 2018. This is a direct result of increased recycling rates.

Recent Waste Statistics

In the United States in 2018, the total generation of waste was 292.4 million tons, or 4.9 pounds per person per day. Of this, more than 146 million tons, around 50%, were landfilled. Currently, the United States is the #1 trash-producing country in the world; 5% of the world's population generates 40% of the world's waste.
